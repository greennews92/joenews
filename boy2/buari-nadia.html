Who is Nadia Buari?

Ghanaian actress, producer, and businesswoman Nadia Buari is a multiple award winner. She has acted in more than 100 films and TV series, and she has received multiple honors for her efforts, such as the 2010 African Movie Academy Awards' Best Actress in a Leading Role.

On November 21, 1982, Buari was born in Ghana's Sekondi-Takoradi. She started her acting career in 2005 and became well-known very soon for her parts in well-known Ghanaian movies like "Mummy's Daughter" and "Beyonce: The President's Daughter.". Her first film, "The Diary of Imogen Brown," was a critical and financial success in 2013.

Buari is a successful entrepreneur in addition to her acting career. The Nadia Buari Foundation is a non-profit organization that promotes the education of underprivileged children in Ghana. She is the founder and CEO of the organization. She is also the creator and CEO of the hair care brand NB Hair.

In Ghana and throughout Africa, Buari serves as an inspiration to a large number of youth. She is a powerful supporter of women's empowerment and education, and she serves as an inspiration to aspiring actors and actresses.
